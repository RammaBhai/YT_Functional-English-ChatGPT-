past + present + perfect

main link := indefinite + continuous + perfect + perfect continuous

-tenses:
- perfect tense
- past tense
- future tense

auxiliary verbs:
- do/does      - did/does   - will/shall
- is/are/am    - was/were   - be/is/are
- has/have     - had/have   - have/has

eg: she does not learn English.
from(1,2,3): learn, learning, learned

// Verb forms
indefinite → mv1  
continuous → mv1 + ing  
perfect → mv3  

⭐ Present Indefinite Tense (habit, schedule, routine)

1. Affirmative [subject + mv1 + s/es (3rd person singular) + object]  
    eg:
    1. I write on the whiteboard.  
    2. You know him/her.  
    3. Haris lives with me.  

2. Negative [subject + do/does + not + mv1 + object]  
    eg:
    1. I do not tell lies.  
    2. She does not take medicines.  
    3. They do not play football.  
    4. She does not try.  

3. Interrogative [do/does + subject + mv1 + object]  
    eg:
    1. Do you play football?  
    2. Do you live there?  
    3. Does she work here?  
    4. Does he know me?  
    5. Does Haris come today?  
    6. Does he teach you?  

4. Double Interrogative (Yes/No) [wh-family + do/does + subject + mv1 + object]  
    eg:
    1. Why do you cry?  
    2. What does he say?  
    3. Where do we go?  

⭐ Present Continuous Tense [subject + is/are/am + mv1 + ing + object]

1. Affirmative  
    eg:  
    1. I am going.  
    2. I am from Dhale.  
    3. She is preparing for the exam.  
    4. You are thinking about it.  

2. Negative  
    eg:  
    1. I am not going.  
    2. She is not preparing for the exam.  
    3. Your brother is not calling you.  
    4. He is not getting me.  

3. Interrogative [is/are/am + subject + mv1 + ing + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Are you talking to me?  
    2. Is she going home?  
    3. Are you celebrating your birthday?  

4. Double Interrogative [wh-family + is/are/am + subject + mv1 + ing + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Where are you going tonight?  
    2. Why is he not talking to me?  
    3. Which phone are you using?  

⭐ Present Perfect Tense [subject + has/have + mv3 + object]

1. Affirmative  
    eg:  
    1. I have eaten the food.  
    2. We have met her/him.  
    3. He has met you.  

2. Negative  
    eg:  
    1. I have not eaten the food.  
    2. We have not met her/him.  
    3. He has not met you.  

3. Interrogative [has/have + subject + mv3 + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Have you won the match?  
    2. Have you done the work?  

4. Double Interrogative [wh-family + has/have + subject + mv3 + object]  
    eg:  
    1. When have you done the work?  

⭐ Present Perfect Continuous Tense [subject + has/have been + mv1 + ing + object + since/for + date/time]

1. Affirmative  
    eg:  
    1. I have been eating the food.  
    2. We have been meeting her/him.  
    3. He has been meeting you.  
    4. I have been waiting for you since morning.  
    5. He has been trying for 2 days.  

2. Negative  
    eg:  
    1. I have not been eating the food.  
    2. Your son has not been coming to school for 2 days.  
    3. She has not been learning her lessons since evening.  

3. Interrogative  
    eg:  
    1. Have you been waiting for me since morning?  
    2. Has she been doing her work since evening?  
    3. Have they been playing football for 2 hours?  

4. Double Interrogative  
    eg:  
    1. Where have you been going since morning?  
    2. Why has she been crying since evening?  

⭐ Past Indefinite Tense [subject + mv2 + object]

1. Affirmative  
    eg:  
    1. I wrote a letter.  
    2. You knew him/her.  
    3. Haris lived with me.  
    4. He came.  
    5. We met him.  

2. Negative [subject + did + not + mv1 + object]  
    eg:  
    1. I did not tell a lie.  
    2. She did not take medicines.  
    3. They did not play football.  

3. Interrogative [did + subject + mv1 + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Did you play?  
    2. Did you live there?  
    3. Did she work here?  

4. Double Interrogative [wh-family + did + subject + mv1 + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Why did you call me?  
    2. What did you ask him?  

⭐ Past Continuous Tense [subject + was/were + mv1 + ing + object]  

1. Affirmative  
    eg:  
    1. I was going.  
    2. She was preparing for the exam.  
    3. You were thinking about it.  

2. Negative  
    eg:  
    1. I was not going.  
    2. She was not taking medicines.  
    3. They were not playing football.  

3. Interrogative [was/were + subject + mv1 + ing + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Was I talking to you?  
    2. Were you speaking English?  

4. Double Interrogative [wh-family + was/were + subject + mv1 + ing + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Where were you going tonight?  
    2. Why was he not talking to me?  

⭐ Past Perfect Tense [subject + had + mv3 + object]

1. Affirmative  
    eg:  
    1. I had eaten the food.  
    2. We had met her/him.  
    3. He had met you.  

2. Negative  
    eg:  
    1. I had not eaten the food.  
    2. We had not met her/him.  
    3. He had not met you.  

3. Interrogative [had + subject + mv3 + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Had you gone there?  
    2. Had you met him?  

4. Double Interrogative [wh-family + had + subject + mv3 + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Where had you gone?  
    2. Why had she been crying?  

⭐ Future Tense (will/shall) [subject + will/shall + mv1 + object]

1. Affirmative  
    eg:  
    1. I will go.  
    2. I shall go.  
    3. You will come.  

2. Negative  
    eg:  
    1. I will not go.  
    2. I shall not go.  
    3. You will not come.  

3. Interrogative [will/shall + subject + mv1 + object]  
    eg:  
    1. Will you go?  
    2. Shall I go?  

4. Double Interrogative [wh-family + will/shall + subject + mv1 + object]  
    eg:  
    1. When will you go?  
    2. Why shall I come?
